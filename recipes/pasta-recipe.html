Savoring Nostalgia: Spaghetti Carbonara

Published on January 5, 2023

There are dishes that transcend mere meals, becoming portals to cherished memories. For me, that dish is Spaghetti Carbonara. This classic Italian recipe isn't just about the perfect balance of pasta, eggs, cheese, and pancetta—it's a vessel for reminiscence.

Each time I prepare Spaghetti Carbonara, I'm transported back to an Italian trattoria nestled in the heart of Rome. The aroma of sizzling pancetta, the anticipation as the pasta cooks to al dente perfection, and the ritual of blending eggs and cheese together—all weave together the tapestry of a cherished memory.

It was a breezy evening; the sun was setting, casting a golden hue over the cobblestone streets. Seated at a quaint table, twirling forkfuls of creamy carbonara, I felt an indescribable sense of contentment. Laughter mingled with the clinking of glasses, and every bite was a celebration of flavors, friendship, and the magic of Rome.

Spaghetti Carbonara isn't just a dish; it's an ode to moments frozen in time, evoking the joy of indulging in simple pleasures and the magic of culinary journeys. So, as I prepare this recipe, I invite you to join me in creating your own treasured memories, one forkful at a time.

Buon appetito!



Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients:


  • 350g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Pecorino Romano cheese (or Parmesan)
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat a drizzle of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or bacon and cook until it's crispy and golden.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese. Season with a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it, reserving a cup of the pasta cooking water.
  5. Add the hot, drained pasta to the pan with the pancetta or bacon. Toss well to coat the pasta with the rendered fat.
  6. Remove the pan from the heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing quickly to combine. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and form a creamy sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta water.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with extra cheese and black pepper if desired.
